# RaWaLLMConfig Contributor License Agreement Policy

RaWaLLMConfig uses AGPL-3.0-or-later for the public source release.

To keep later monetization and dual-licensing options possible, external
contributions require a Contributor License Agreement (CLA) before merge.

## Current Policy

- External contributions are not merged unless the contributor has accepted
  the RaWaLLMConfig CLA.
- The CLA must grant the project owner broad, irrevocable rights to use,
  reproduce, modify, distribute, sublicense, and relicense the contribution.
- The public project license remains AGPL-3.0-or-later unless explicitly
  changed by the project owner.
- The CLA does not grant trademark rights to use the RaWaLLMConfig name,
  logo, or confusingly similar branding.

## Why This Exists

A Developer Certificate of Origin (DCO) can confirm that a contributor has the
right to submit a change under the project license. For this project, that is
not enough on its own, because future commercial builds or dual-license offers
may require explicit relicensing rights for third-party contributions.

## Operational Rule

If a pull request or patch comes from a contributor without a completed CLA,
maintainers should ask for the CLA step first and leave the contribution
unmerged.

This file is a release policy, not legal advice. Before accepting substantial
external contributions, the project owner should use a reviewed CLA workflow or
legal template that matches the intended commercial model.
